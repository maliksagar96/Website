FDTD Allen Taflove Chapter 1

Primary reasons for using FVTD:

  1. No Linear Algebra involved.
  2. Accurate and robust. Errors that occur in FDTD models are well understood and can be bounded to provide accurate results.
  3. Can calculate non-linear behaviours very easily.
  4. Doesn't require calculation of any structure-dependent Green's function.
  5. Easy to visualise.

ABCs in the FDTD method allow waves to exit the domain with almost negligible reflections. Leapfrog method is used. Very friendly to parallel computing.

We will now write the wave equation in the discrete form:

\( \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \)

The discretised equation looks like:br

\(u_i^{n+1} = (c\Delta t)^2(u^n_{i+1} - 2u_i^n + u_{i-1}^n) + 2u^n_i - u_i^{n-1}\)
